The advent of social media has significantly altered the landscape of modern friendships. On one hand, platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter have made it easier for people to connect with others across geographical distances, fostering a sense of global community. Social media has also enabled individuals to maintain relationships that may have otherwise fizzled out due to physical distance or busy schedules. However, the rise of social media has also been criticized for contributing to the erosion of deep, meaningful friendships. The curated and often superficial nature of online interactions can create unrealistic expectations and promote comparison rather than genuine connection. Furthermore, the constant stream of updates and notifications can lead to a sense of overwhelm and distraction, making it challenging for people to engage in meaningful, in-person interactions. As a result, many people are left feeling isolated and disconnected, despite being constantly connected to others through their screens.
